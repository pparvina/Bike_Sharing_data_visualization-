Bike-Sharing System Characteristics¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at characteristics of bike-sharing system that could be used to predict their most use.The main focus was on the duration of the trip, user types, the days and the hours of the week when bike sharing was most used.

Dataset Overview¶

This data set includes information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area. Varibales include trip duration, start time and date,start station name, user type.

Research questions:¶

  • When are most trips taken in terms of time of day and day of the week?
  • How long does the average trip take?
  • Does the above depend on if a user is a subscriber or customer?

Distribution of Bike User Types¶

The plot shows the Subscribers are the main users of bike sharing system. The definition of user types is following: “Subscriber” = Member or “Customer” = Casual.

Distribution of Bike-Sharing by Member's Gender¶

Majority of users are male.

Distribution of Members SWho Used Bike-Sharing System for All Trip¶

Only abour 20000 of users utilize bike sharing service for their whole trip while over 160000 do not use bike sharing system for all trip.

User Type and Member's Gender¶

Among members who are customers majority of users are male while the similar characteristics prevail in subscriber category as well.

Most Used Stations¶

Market St at 10th St and San Fransico Caltrain Station 2 are major stations where trips started.

Bike-Sharing Used for All Trip¶

Eventhough majority of people do not complete their whole trip with bikes, but the ones who complete their whole trip said they use bike-sharing for about 10 minutes.

The Trips Distribution Over hour-of-day and day-of-week¶

Thursdays and Tuesday had the highest number of bike sharing trips happening and the 8-9 am and 5-6 pm were the peak hours when the service was used.

Distribution by user type, day of the week and duration of the trip¶

Busiest days for bike sharing are Sunday and Saturdays for both user types. The duration of the trips were longer for customers between 25~30 mins in average while for subcribers it was about 10 minutes.

Distribution of Trip Duration by User Type¶

The below graph validates initial findings where we saw there are more subscribers using bike sharing service than customers.For both user types the trips were about 10-20 minutes on average.

Insights¶

  • I found that Subscribers are the main users of bike sharing system. So, the bike sharing system can target customer user type to expand their consumer segment.
  • The majority of users are male. For reference, Bike sharing companies could target female users in the future and engage them in using bike sharing services.
  • Actually, by plotting the start_station and the end_station I was able to identify the two major stations that bike users start and end their trip. The above results show that San Francisco Caltrain Station 2 and Market St at 10th St are the two major stations; these stations are probably the center of the city thus a lot of the users start or end their trips here and perhaps if the Bay Wheels would like to gain higher number of users can increase number of available bike sharing service in those stations.
  • Another observation from above earlier we found out that the most trips started at 8-9 am and 5-6 pm and ended in the same time slots.
  • The above plots show that busiest days for bike sharing are Sunday and Saturdays with the duration of the trips between 25~30 mins in average.
